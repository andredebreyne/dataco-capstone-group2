# Table 7. AO3 Operational Recommendation Matrix

Source artifacts: `data/references/ao3_operational_recommendation_matrix.csv`, `docs/ao3_operational_recommendations.md`, `docs/ao3_methodology_and_results.md`.

| AO3 segment | Operational priority | Logistics action | Pricing/profitability action | Monitoring action | Caution/limitation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `protect_high_value_at_risk` | Primary protection queue | Review carrier/service capacity early, confirm dispatch readiness, and escalate preventable delay risks. | Preserve expected margin by avoiding unnecessary blanket expediting; use targeted intervention when delay risk is actionable. | Track count, share, average risk, expected profit, and predicted margin; monitor intervention volume. | Recommendations are limited to predicted risk and predicted margin evidence; they do not establish realized delivery or profit improvement. |
| `expedite_selectively` | Controlled exception review | Investigate root causes and intervene only when customer impact or SLA exposure justifies the cost. | Avoid automatic premium-cost expediting because average predicted profit and margin are negative. | Track as a small exception queue and compare intervention costs against expected value. | Small segment size means recommendations should be applied case by case and not generalized as a broad operating policy. |
| `preserve_service` | Service preservation | Keep normal dispatch quality controls and watch for emerging delay indicators. | Protect margin by avoiding avoidable intervention cost; do not over-service solely because margin is high. | Track high-margin volume, service consistency, and any drift toward higher risk. | Low predicted risk does not remove the need for normal monitoring and only argues against urgent intervention by default. |
| `standard_process` | Routine processing with margin monitoring | Keep routine handling and exception management only when operational alerts emerge. | Monitor pricing or cost drivers because average predicted profit and margin are negative. | Track count, share, negative margin exposure, and operational drift. | Small segment size limits broad conclusions; recommendations should remain conservative. |
| `requires_score_review` | Data-quality exception | Review only if an external operational alert exists; do not infer risk from missing score fields. | Review data and scoring availability before any margin-driven action. | Track missing-score count and investigate upstream scoring failures. | If this segment appears in future refreshes it indicates a data or scoring issue, not a direct business recommendation. |
| `requires_margin_review` | Data-quality exception | Review only if independent operational risk evidence exists; do not use the AO3 margin axis until corrected. | Resolve nonpositive or missing order value and margin inputs before pricing or margin action. | Track invalid-margin count and investigate upstream AO2 or order-value issues. | If this segment appears in future refreshes it indicates a data-quality issue and should require manual review before business action. |
